Art Arcade

Project Synopsis

The critical aim of Art Arcade is to present a platform to question the conventions surrounding interaction with art at present; exploring the potential for work to exist at the intersection between high and low culture.

The project encompasses an open call for proposals, selection of participants by the lead artists, workshops and meetings for development of a body of work, and will culminate in an exhibition consisting of coin operated artworks and artworks for sale in vending machines.

The individual pieces by each artist will be developed in dialogue with the other participants through workshops and subsequent meetings.

Collaborations and Workshops

While the proposals for the Art Arcade can be either individual or collaborative, the workshops and meetings throughout the duration of the project will revolve around the presentation of work in progress, hands-on technical tutorials, and the exchange of ideas and strategies between all of the artists participating in the project.

Artists that don't typically work with electronics are encouraged to submit proposals. The technical background of the lead artists will be utilized as a means of support for all participating artists wishing to integrate electronics and kinetic components to their practice.

Curated Vending Machines

Alongside the main works, all participants are invited to submit artworks for the various vending machines that will also be featured in the show.

The design of a vending machine lends itself to collaborative efforts within an art context as it is often a central source for various products. There will be various formats of vending machine, from gumball dispensers to soda machines. These will determine the possible size and weight of the artworks.
